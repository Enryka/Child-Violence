Why we do what we do?

Mistaken Witness Identification (MWID)

In 26% of the exonerations listed by the NRE, mistaken witness identification (MWID) contributed to the wrongful conviction of an innocent person.31

Perjury or False Accusation (P/FA)

62% of exonerations documented by the National Registry of Exonerations involved perjury or false accusations.42

False Confessions (FC)

False primary confessions, as opposed to false secondary confessions, have been involved in approximately 12% of all known exonerations, and about 28% of DNA-based exonerations according to the NRE.47

alse or Misleading Forensic Evidence (F/MFE)

Roughly 23% of known exonerations have involved false or misleading forensic science (F/MFE).51.

Official Misconduct (OM)

Official misconduct (OM) has contributed to 57% of known exonerations.

Inadequate Legal Defense (ILD)

Approximately 26% of exonerees had an inadequate legal defense (ILD).
